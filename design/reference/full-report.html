About the 993 turbo
Representative 993 Turbo · Photo: Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA 4.0
The last of the air-cooled. Produced 1995–1998, the 993 Turbo introduced twin turbochargers and all-wheel drive on the chassis collectors regard as the most sorted air-cooled 911 ever built. Hand-built in Stuttgart, with a six-speed manual transaxle as the only transmission offered.
01
Coolant tube failures
Plastic factory tubes degrade with age. Aluminum upgrade is $3K–5K if not done.
02
Secondary air injection
Clogged passages trigger CEL and emissions failures. Repair requires head removal.
03
Turbo system longevity
K16 turbos are durable when serviced. Listen for whistles or shaft play.
04
Originality drift
Many were modified. Non-stock turbos, ECUs, and exhausts can affect value 10–20%.

The 993 Turbo and the End of an Era
Comparison test against the contemporary 911 Turbo S placed the 993 Turbo at the top of the air-cooled lineage. Noted the steering feedback as unmatched by anything water-cooled that came after.
