31-Years-Owned 1988 Porsche 911 Carrera Coupe G50

This 1988 Porsche 911 Carrera coupe was sold new at Hoehn Motors of Carlsbad, California, and has remained registered in that state through the current owner’s purchase in 1995. Specified in Venetian Blue Metallic over blue leather upholstery, the car was optioned with sport shocks and a short shifter. It is powered by a 3.2-liter flat-six paired with a G50 five-speed manual transaxle and features 16″ Fuchs alloy wheels, a power-operated sunroof, power-adjustable front seats, a Blaupunkt cassette stereo, a HiFi sound system, cruise control, and air conditioning. This Carrera coupe is offered by the seller, a BaT Local Partner, with a Porsche Production Specifications sheet, manufacturer’s literature, service records, a clean Carfax report, and a clean California title in the owner’s name.

The car was finished from the factory in Venetian Blue Metallic and was refinished in 1999. Exterior details include a power-operated sunroof, integrated fog lights, and heated power-adjustable side mirrors. Close-up photographs of the exterior finish and trim are shown in the gallery below.

The 16″ Fuchs alloy wheels are fitted with Continental ExtremeContact tires that were mounted in 2023. The car was optioned with sport shocks, and a front strut tower brace has been added. Braking is handled by four-wheel discs.

The cabin features power-adjustable front seats upholstered in blue leather, and amenities include a short shifter, a HiFi sound system, a Blaupunkt cassette stereo, cruise control, air conditioning, and power windows.

The leather-wrapped steering wheel frames VDO instrumentation, including a central tachometer with a 6,300-rpm redline as well as a 170-mph speedometer, a Quarz clock, and gauges for oil level, oil pressure, oil temperature, and fuel level. The six-digit odometer shows 123k miles, approximately 59k miles of which were added under current ownership. The odometer and tachometer were repaired by Palo Alto Speedometer in California in 2018 and 2020, respectively.

The 3.2-liter M930/25 flat-six features Bosch Motronic engine management and was factory rated at 217 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque.  In 2024, approximately 1,100 miles ago, the valves were adjusted, and the spark plugs, oxygen sensor, valve cover gaskets, and an oil line were replaced. Compression test results from that time are shown in the gallery below. A Weltmeister ECU chip has been added, and the car last passed a California emissions test in September 2024.

Power is sent to the rear wheels through a G50 five-speed manual transaxle. The shifter bushings were replaced in September 2023, and corrosion is noted around the exhaust.

The Porsche Production Specifications sheet shows factory colors, equipment, and a production completion date of July 16, 1987.

Service records, manufacturer’s literature, and a handwritten fuel log will accompany the sale.

The Carfax report is free of accidents or other reported damage from its first entry in January 1991.
