This 2026 Porsche 911 GT3 was ordered with a six-speed manual transaxle in addition to 20″ and 21″ forged lightweight magnesium wheels and the $20k Weissach Package, which included lightweight door panels, Weissach embroidery on the headrests, a dashboard-mounted Weissach badge, carbon-fiber body components, a shortened GT-specific gear lever, and a carbon-fiber rear anti-roll bar and rear underbody panel. Finished in Lugano Blue over black leather and Race-Tex, the car is powered by a 4.0-liter flat-six and is further equipped with a Porsche Torque Vectoring Plus electronically locking differential, HD-Matrix Design LED headlights, the Chrono Package, a swan-neck rear wing, rear-axle steering, a front-axle lift system, heated carbon-fiber bucket seats, a Bose sound system, and Porsche Communication Management with navigation. Acquired by the selling dealer in 2025, this 992.2 GT3 has 61 miles and is now offered in California with a window sticker, the owner’s manual, a clean Carfax report, and a clean New York title.

The car is finished in Lugano Blue with exposed carbon-fiber stripes on the hood, roof, and swan-neck rear wing, and additional details include HD-Matrix Design LED headlights, rear parking sensors, a rear diffuser, Exclusive Design taillights, puddle light projectors, and twin center-mounted exhaust outlets.

The 20″ and 21″ forged lightweight magnesium wheels were specified as a $17k option, and they are mounted with Michelin tires. The car is equipped with rear-axle steering, a front-axle lift system, and Porsche Active Suspension Management (PASM), which included a 10mm lower ride height. Braking is handled by yellow-finished Porsche-script calipers over Porsche Ceramic Composite rotors. The Weissach Package added a carbon-fiber rear anti-roll bar and coupling rods.

The heated carbon-fiber bucket seats are upholstered in black leather and Race-Tex with Shark Blue stitching and are complemented by a Race-Tex-wrapped upper dashboard, a Weissach Package logo on the passenger-side dashboard, and Weissach headrest embroidery. Cabin appointments include a shortened GT-specific gear lever, carbon-fiber trim, cruise control, dual-zone automatic climate control, a Bose sound system, a fire extinguisher, ambient lighting, and Porsche Communication Management with navigation.

The Race-Tex-wrapped multifunction steering wheel features a drive-mode dial, and it fronts a configurable digital gauge cluster. A Sport Chrono stopwatch is mounted atop the center of the dash. The digital odometer indicates 61 miles.

The 4.0-liter flat-six features a 9k-rpm redline and was factory rated at 502 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque. The car is also equipped with an extended-range fuel tank.

Power is routed to the rear wheels through a six-speed manual transaxle and a Porsche Torque Vectoring Plus electronically locking differential. The car is further equipped with a carbon-fiber underbody panel.

The window sticker shows initial delivery to Porsche Boston in Natick, Massachusetts, along with factory colors, options, and a total price of $308,430.

The Carfax report is free of accidents or other reported damage.
