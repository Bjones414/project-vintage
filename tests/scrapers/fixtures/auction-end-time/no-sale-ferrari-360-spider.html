This Ferrari 360 got away, but there are more like it here.

2001 Ferrari 360 Spider

USA
Bid to USD $53,000 on 11/7/2528Comments
View Result
MakeFerrari
ModelFerrari 360
Era2000s
OriginItalian
CategoryConvertibles
LocationLocated in United States

This 2001 Ferrari 360 Spider is powered by a 3.6-liter V8 paired with a six-speed F1 sequential transaxle and is finished in Giallo Modena over Nero leather. Equipment includes a limited-slip differential, 18″ five-spoke alloy wheels, a black convertible soft top, xenon headlights, Scuderia Ferrari fender shields, a Challenge-style rear grille, power-adjustable seats, and automatic climate control as well as a Jensen head unit with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto connectivity. This 360 Spider has 36k miles and is now offered by the selling dealer with a clean California title.

The Pininfarina-designed aluminum body is finished in Giallo Modena and features xenon headlights, Scuderia Ferrari fender shields, a power-retractable black convertible soft top, a Challenge-style rear grille, and quad exhaust outlets exiting through the rear fascia. The selling dealer advises that the soft top requires adjustment due to misalignment when opening.

The 18″ five-spoke alloy wheels are mounted with Falken Azenis FK450 A/S tires. Stopping power is from red-finished Ferrari-branded calipers over cross-drilled and ventilated rotors at each corner.

The cockpit features power-adjustable Daytona-style seats that are trimmed in Nero leather with yellow piping. A Jensen head unit with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto has been fitted, and additional amenities include aluminum pedals and automatic climate control. The selling dealer notes that the air conditioning does not blow cold.

The leather-wrapped steering wheel frames a 220-mph speedometer and a tachometer with an 8,500-rpm redline as well as a digital fuel level readout and gauges for oil pressure, oil temperature, and coolant temperature. Paddle shifters for the F1 gearbox are mounted behind the steering wheel. The digital odometer indicates 36k miles.

The mid-mounted 3.6-liter Tipo F131 V8 features a flat-plane crankshaft, dry-sump lubrication, dual overhead camshafts, and five valves per cylinder. Factory-rated output was 395 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque.

Power is sent to the rear wheels through an electrohydraulic six-speed F1 sequential transaxle and a limited-slip differential.

The Carfax report shows no accidents or damage.
