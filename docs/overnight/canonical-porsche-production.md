## Notes on Using This Document

- **PS vs. hp**: German manufacturers quote DIN PS. 1 PS ≈ 0.9863 hp (SAE). Where both figures are in the literature, both are given.
- **Production figures** for older models frequently vary 2–5% across sources due to counting methodology (chassis built vs. cars delivered, including prototypes vs. not). Discrepancies are noted.
- **MSRP** figures are US market unless stated otherwise, adjusted to the model year quoted.
- **Body styles**: Coupé, Cabriolet (Cab), Targa, Roadster, Speedster, Spyder are abbreviated in tables.

## 1. 356 Family (1948–1965)

### Family Overview

The 356 was Porsche's founding production car, built first in Gmünd, Austria (1948–1950) and then at the Reutter coachworks in Stuttgart-Zuffenhausen. All 356s use a rear-mounted, air-cooled flat-four engine derived from Volkswagen components, progressively replaced by Porsche-designed castings. The four-cam Carrera engine (Type 547) is a distinct lineage within the 356 family.

Total 356 production (all series): **78,316** units. [Source: elferspot.com, citing Porsche AG production records]

## 4. 911 Early / F-Series (1963–1973)

### Family Overview

Long-wheelbase (2,271 mm) replaced short-wheelbase (2,204 mm) from August 1968 (E-series).
