# Cayenne + Panamera — Family Reference Doc

*Research compiled 2026-05-18. [VERIFY] = uncertain; do not treat as authoritative without corroboration against Porsche AG press materials or Porsche Archiv records.*

---

## Summary

**Cayenne (2003–present):** Porsche's first SUV, co-developed on the VW Group PL71 / MLB / MLB Evo platforms. Now the brand's best-selling nameplate globally. Five internal-code generations: **955 → 957 → 958 → 9Y0 → 9YA (2024 facelift)**. The catalog collapses 955+957 into `cayenne-i` and 9Y0+9YA into `cayenne-iii`.

**Panamera (2010–present):** Four-door sport saloon with optional Sport Turismo wagon and Executive long-wheelbase body. Three internal-code generations: **970 → 971 → 972 (2024 facelift)**. The catalog collapses 971+972 into `panamera-ii`.

**Critical catalog gaps across both families:**
- Panamera S (V8/V6T RWD) absent from both 970 and 971 generations
- Diesel and S Diesel entirely absent from all Cayenne gens where they were offered
- 958 S Hybrid (non-PHEV) missing; only PHEV E-Hybrid is in catalog
- Cayenne Transsyberia (957 special edition) absent
- Panamera Sport Turismo wagon body absent from panamera-ii (significant auction volume)
- Panamera Executive LWB absent from both 970 and 971
- Cayenne Coupé body sub-trims (S Coupé, GTS Coupé, E-Hybrid Coupé) absent from cayenne-iii
- Turbo S (standalone ICE) status in 9Y0/cayenne-iii is unresolved [VERIFY]
- 9YA and 972 need generation splits from their parent entries for comp accuracy

Catalog gen count: **3 Cayenne + 2 Panamera = 5 entries** covering 50+ real-world factory trim lines.

### C — Cayenne Gaps

1. **955/957 conflation in `cayenne-i`** — The 957 introduced a new 4.8L V8 family, the GTS trim, and significantly different power outputs. Comp matching a 2003 955 Turbo (450 PS) against a 2009 957 Turbo (500 PS) produces misleading results. Recommend splitting `cayenne-i` into `cayenne-955` (2003–2007) and `cayenne-957` (2008–2010).

2. **Diesel absent from all generations** — `'Diesel'` is missing from `cayenne-i` (957 era) and `cayenne-ii` (958 era). EU-sourced cars appear frequently on BaT and Collecting Cars. Add to both gen entries.

3. **S Diesel (958) absent** — Distinct from base Diesel; 4.2L V8 TDI, 385 PS. High-performance diesel with meaningfully different auction value. Add `'S Diesel'` to cayenne-ii.

4. **Transsyberia (957) absent** — Factory-approved special edition, ~500 units [VERIFY]. Rally-themed; distinct suspension spec. Not in catalog; add `'Transsyberia'` to cayenne-i (or cayenne-957 if split).

5. **S Hybrid (958.1) absent** — Non-PHEV hybrid (MY2012–2013) is not the same car as the later PHEV E-Hybrid (MY2014+). Currently only `'E-Hybrid'` is in cayenne-ii. Add `'S Hybrid'` for 958.1 era.

6. **Turbo S E-Hybrid (cayenne-ii) — unresolved** — Audit flags whether this trim existed in 958 or is 9Y0-only. Resolve before adding to cayenne-ii.

7. **Turbo S (cayenne-iii ICE) — unresolved** — The standalone non-hybrid Turbo S in 9Y0 is disputed. If it does not exist as a factory trim, remove from cayenne-iii. The flagship is the Turbo S E-Hybrid.

8. **Coupé sub-trims absent** — `cayenne-iii` has `'Coupé'` and `'Turbo Coupé'` but is missing S Coupé, GTS Coupé, E-Hybrid Coupé, Turbo S E-Hybrid Coupé. All are confirmed or likely factory trims for MY2020+. Verify and add. Comp engine also needs to apply a body-style flag since Coupé vs. SUV trade at different price points with identical drivetrains.

9. **9YA generation split needed** — The 2024 facelift restructured powertrain tiers. Grouping 2019–2023 9Y0 cars with 2024+ 9YA cars will cause comp quality degradation. Recommend splitting `cayenne-iii` at 2024.

10. **GTS year-start constraint** — GTS was added MY2013 in 958 and MY2021 in 9Y0. Catalog year ranges allow invalid 2011 or 2018 Cayenne GTS queries. Year-aware trim validation should enforce this; `isValidCombination` currently does not cover sub-trim year gaps.

### C — Panamera Gaps

1. **Panamera S (V8/V6T RWD) missing from both generations** — The "Panamera S" (RWD; 400–420 PS depending on year) is a factory production trim absent from both `panamera-i` and `panamera-ii`. Comp engine will misclassify 970 S auction results as the V6 "Base" model. Add `'S'` to both generations. Confirm US market availability for 971-era S before adding; may be RoW-only.

2. **Panamera S Hybrid (970, MY2012–2013) missing** — Non-PHEV parallel hybrid with 380 PS; distinct from the later PHEV "4 E-Hybrid" (416 PS). Not in catalog. Add to `panamera-i` as `'S Hybrid'`.

3. **Sport Turismo body entirely absent from panamera-ii** — All 971 Sport Turismo variants (2018+) are missing. Wagon body commands a distinct valuation versus the sedan in EU and Asian markets. Add `'Sport Turismo'` to `panamera-ii` or add body-style sub-trims. The 4 E-Hybrid Sport Turismo in particular has high EU BaT/Collecting Cars volume.

4. **Executive LWB absent from both generations** — Present on the real-world lineup since MY2014 (970) and throughout 971. Not in either catalog gen. Add to both.

5. **972 generation split needed** — The 972 has a restructured powertrain (E-Hybrid replaces S tier; Turbo E-Hybrid replaces Turbo S E-Hybrid effectively). Pairing 2024 972 cars against 2017 971 cars will produce low-confidence comps. Split `panamera-ii` at MY2024 into `panamera-971` (2017–2023) and `panamera-972` (2024–present).

6. **GTS year-start (970) — invalid queries possible** — The 970 GTS was added MY2012. A query for "2010 Panamera GTS" is impossible but catalog allows it. Year-aware trim validation gap; same pattern as Cayenne GTS and other mid-gen additions.

7. **971.2 Tech facelift (MY2021) not distinguished** — The 971.2 introduced engine updates affecting the Turbo. Splitting at MY2021 would improve 971 comp reliability, though it is lower priority than the 972 split.

8. **Catalog year boundary: panamera-i ends 2016 / panamera-ii starts 2017** — This is correct per the 971 launch. No fix needed here.
