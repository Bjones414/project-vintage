## Summary

The 996 (internal type 996) is the first water-cooled 911, produced model years 1998–2005. It divides cleanly at the 2002 facelift.

**996.1 (MY1998–2001):** Introduced water-cooling with a new 3.4L M96 engine. Distinctive "fried-egg" headlights shared with the 986 Boxster. Core lineup: Carrera and Carrera 4 coupe + cabriolet, Turbo Coupe, GT3 Mk1 (Europe/ROW only). GT2 Mk1 appeared at or near the end of this phase (MY2001–2002 depending on market).

**996.2 (MY2002–2005):** Facelift with distinct projector headlights and restyled front/rear. Engine grew to 3.6L (M96/03, 320PS). New additions: Carrera 4S (wide Turbo body, AWD), Targa, Turbo Cabriolet, Turbo S, GT3 Mk2, GT3 RS, and the 40 Jahre 911 special edition (MY2004). GT2 continues as Mk2.

**Engine architecture split — the most important fact about this generation:**

| Engine | Cars | IMS Bearing Risk | AOS Risk | RMS Leak Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| M96/01 (3.4L) | 996.1 Carrera, Carrera 4, Carrera Cabriolet variants | YES | YES | YES |
| M96/03 (3.6L) | 996.2 Carrera, Carrera 4, Carrera 4S, Targa | YES | YES | YES |
| Mezger (GT1-derived, 3.6L) | Turbo, Turbo S, GT3, GT3 RS, GT2 | NO | NO | NO |

**IMS clarity for 996:** The 996.1 (pre-facelift) used a larger dual-row IMS bearing in early production, transitioning to a single-row bearing approximately MY2000 onward. ALL 996.2 cars use the single-row IMS. There was no IMS improvement with the 996.2 facelift — the bearing change happened within the 996.1 run and carried into 996.2. The Turbo, GT3, and GT2 are unaffected (Mezger engine).

## Section C — Gaps and Issues

### C1 — Phantom Trim in Catalog: "Carrera S" (996.2)

**The 996 generation never had a production "Carrera S" trim.**

The `models.ts` 996.2 entry includes `'Carrera S'` in its trim array. This trim does not correspond to a Porsche factory production model for the 996. The Carrera S designation — denoting a rear-wheel-drive car with the wide Turbo body — was introduced with the **997 generation (2005)**. For the 996.2, the wide-body RWD option did not exist as a standard catalog model; the wide-body options were Carrera 4S (AWD), Turbo (AWD), Turbo S (AWD), and GT2 (RWD, but a separate performance category).

**Action required:** Remove `'Carrera S'` from the 996.2 trim array. Any existing `listings` rows with `trim = 'Carrera S'` and `generation = '996.2'` should be flagged for manual review.

---

### C2 — 996.2 yearEnd in Catalog is Wrong

The catalog shows `yearEnd: 2004` for 996.2. The 996 was produced through **MY2005** (final production ended in 2005). The GT3 RS, Turbo S Cabriolet, and GT2 final cars were delivered as MY2005. This is not a gray area — it is a data error.

**Action required:** `yearEnd` for `996.2` should be `2005`.

---

Cabriolets are not cosmetic variants — they have different structural weight, different top-speed ratings, and distinct production counts that affect comp engine accuracy. They should be separate trims.

### C4 — GT2 Generation Assignment Needs Verification

The GT2 Mk1 / Mk2 split across 996.1 and 996.2 is assigned per the project brief, but the exact production boundary needs confirmation:

- Some sources date GT2 Mk1 as **MY2001** (introduced at IAA Frankfurt 2001 for 2002 production), which would make it **entirely a 996.2 car** (post-facelift), not 996.1.
- Other sources note that Mk1 GT2 cars were built on the pre-facelift bodyshell through early 2002 production before the facelift tooling was fully deployed.
- The split between "963 Mk1 / 324 Mk2" (per project `production-counts.md`) needs a Porsche AG production log or Porsche Type 996 registry confirmation.

**Action required:** Verify with Porsche AG type 996 production records or marque registry before assigning GT2 to 996.1 catalog. If the GT2 Mk1 was only ever built on the post-facelift body, it belongs entirely in 996.2.

### C5 — GT3 Not Sold in USA — Must Not Comp Against US-Market Cars

Both the GT3 Mk1 and GT3 Mk2 were **never officially sold in the US market.** The GT3 RS was also never US-market. All three are US grey-import (parallel import) cars only.

Consequence for the comp engine: Any comparable-sales filtering that restricts to US-auction results will find near-zero GT3 comps. The comp engine must either (a) allow international auction results for GT3 comparables, or (b) explicitly note in the analysis UI that comparable data is limited by US non-availability.

This is NOT a catalog error — it is an operational flag for the comp engine team. Do not add a "US Mkt: Yes" field for GT3 in the catalog.

### C6 — 40 Jahre 911 Not in Catalog

The 40 Jahre 911 (40 Years 911) is a factory limited edition for MY2004. It is not a Porsche Exclusive custom; it was a named special edition with a dedicated production run (1,963 units, factory-allocated GT Silver Metallic). It should be in the catalog as a distinct trim so it can be correctly identified in `listings` and properly analyzed (same mechanicals as Carrera, but distinct provenance value and collectibility).

**Action required:** Add `'40 Jahre 911'` to the 996.2 trim array. The number 1,963 was chosen to match the year the 911 debuted. [VERIFY: whether it was based on standard Carrera body or offered on 4S / other variants.]

### C7 — IMS Fact-Check: No Improvement at 996.2 Facelift

**Confirm for any editorial content or watch-for data:** There was no IMS bearing improvement introduced with the 996.2 facelift. Some third-party write-ups incorrectly imply the 2002 refresh addressed the IMS. It did not. The transition from dual-row to single-row IMS bearing occurred mid-production within the 996.1 run (approximately MY2000), not at the facelift. ALL 996.2 Carrera/Targa/C4/C4S cars carry the single-row IMS.

If the defect catalog at `docs/reference/defects/01_engine_m96_m97.md` contains any language suggesting the facelift improved the IMS, it must be corrected.

The table above assigns 483 PS to the GT2 Mk2. The 483 PS figure may be a confusion with a later GT2 generation (997 GT2).
