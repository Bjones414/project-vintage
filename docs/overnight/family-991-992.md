# 911 Family — 991.1 / 991.2 / 992.1 / 992.2

*Research compiled 2026-05-18. Uncertain claims tagged [VERIFY]. Do not modify catalog, code, or DB based on this document without review.*

---

## Summary

The 991 and 992 generations span the contemporary 911 era (2012–present). The 991.1 was the last generation with naturally aspirated Carrera engines; the 991.2 turbocharged the standard lineup in 2016. The 992.1 standardized the wide body across all variants and introduced significant limited-edition halo models. The 992.2 facelift (2024) introduced T-Hybrid powertrains on GTS and Turbo S variants — the first hybrid 911 in production.

**Cabriolet note (all sub-generations):** Cabriolet body is available on Carrera, Carrera S, Carrera 4, Carrera 4S, GTS variants, Turbo, and Turbo S. The catalog does not list Cabriolet as separate trim entries — body style is a comp-engine filter, not a trim field. This document follows the same convention unless otherwise noted.

**Combined 991 production:** 233,540 total (991.1 + 991.2 combined) [Source: Porsche AG, via enthusiast registry analysis]. Sub-generation split not publicly confirmed; estimated ~50/50 given equal production windows.

### C.1 — 991.1 Gaps

- **Add `'Targa 4 GTS'`** — Factory production trim, MY2015–2016, 3.8L NA, 430hp, AWD, PDK/7MT. Announced at NAIAS January 2015. Confirmed 991.1 production variant. The catalog has both Targa 4 and Targa 4S but is missing this GTS variant.
